RunningCoach – Cadence Training System for Long-Distance Runners
Daniel Aranki, Uma Balakrishnan, Hannah Sarver, Lucas Serven, Carlos Asuncion, Kaidi Du, Caitlin Gruis, Gao Xian Peh, Yu Xiao, Ruzena Bajcsy
Proceedings of the 11th International Conference on Pervasive Computing Technologies for Healthcare (Health-i-Coach), 2017

In this paper, we present an mHealth system that remotely monitors running cadence levels of runners in a continuous fashion, among other variables, and provides immediate feedback to runners in an effort to help them optimize their running cadence. We also present some initial findings based on a feasibility study we are currently conducting using this system.

The Feasibility and Usability of RunningCoach: a Remote Coaching System for Long-Distance Runners
Daniel Aranki, Gao Xian Peh, Gregorij Kurillo and Ruzena Bajcsy
Sensors: Special Issue "Smart Sensing Technologies for Personalised Coaching"

In this paper, we present the results and user feedback from a feasibility study that we have conducted on RunningCoach. RunningCoach is an mHealth system that remotely monitors running cadence levels of runners in a continuous fashion, among other variables, and provides immediate feedback to runners in an effort to help them optimize their running cadence.

Text Analysis of Hillary Clinton's Emails (Python, Word2Vec, NLP)

This was a collaborative group project together with Jack Kolb and Jong Ha Lee. We were curious about the contents of Hillary Clinton’s emails, so we decided to use Word2Vec to to compute the embeddings of the words contained in her emails and TSNE for dimensionality reduction. Interestingly, the phrase 'Hillary Clinton' is clustered along with 'John McCain' whereas 'Barack Obama' is clustered along with 'Muammar Gaddafi', 'Libya' and 'Benghazi'.
